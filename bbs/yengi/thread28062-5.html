سىزنى ماقالە سۇنۇشقا بىر قېتىم تەكلىپ قىلىپ، ئاندىن تولا سۈيلەپ كۆزىڭىزگە كىرىۋالغان ژورناللارنىڭ ھېچ قايسىسى ئادەتتە ياخشى جورنال ئەمەس.  ئۇلارنىڭ مەقسىدى سىزنىڭ ماقالىڭىزنى ئېلان قىلىپ بېرىش ئەمەس، بەلكى كۆپۈنچە ئەھۋالدا سىزدىن پۇل تېپىش.

ماڭىمۇ ماقالە ئىۋەرتىش ھەققىدىكى تەكلىپلەر پات-پات كېلىپ تۇرىدۇ.  ئەمما بىر ژورنالدىن پەقەت بىر قېتىملا خەت كېلىدۇ.  ئۇنىڭدىن كېيىن ئۇلار مېنى ئىككىنچى سۈيلىمەيدۇ.

The Journal of Astronomical Telescopes, Instruments, and Systems will be publishing a Special Section on Wide-Field Infrared Survey Telescope—Astrophysics Focused Telescope Assets Coronagraphs, guest edited by Olivier Guyon and Motohide Tamura.
The submission deadline is 1 April 2015.
The Wide-Field Infrared Survey Telescope—Astrophysics Focused Telescope Assets (WFIRST-AFTA) coronagraph instrument is a major step in enabling direct imaging of nearby exoplanets. The purpose of this special section is to present an overview of the AFTA coronagraph technologies, as well as related space high-contrast imaging and spectroscopy, wavefront sensing and control, algorithms, modeling, operations, and data analysis.
Related topics of interest include, but are not limited to:
•        AFTA coronagraph technologies
•        Optics and mechanics for high-contrast imaging
•        Methods and algorithms for wavefront sensing and control
•        Structural, thermal, and optical performance models
•        Mission operations
•        Data analysis
•        Science yield: number of planets, spectra quality
•        Laboratory demonstrations
See the full call for papers
Manuscripts should be submitted to SPIE according to the journal guidelines with a cover letter indicating that the submission is intended for this special section. All papers submitted for this special section, both solicited and unsolicited, will go through the standard journal review process.
